Assignment Description

The project consisted of choosing one of the UN’s SDG indicators and creating a choropleth map of it. Making a choropleth map would highlight clearly which nations are achieving these indicators. A choropleth map uses differences in shading, coloring, or the placement of symbols within predefined areas to indicate data values in those areas. The difference in attribute values is typically shown by a visual variable, such as color, which ranges from light to dark. I decided to make a choropleth map covering indicator 5.5.2, which is the proportion of women in managerial positions. This indicator measures the proportion of women in decision-making roles across the executive, legislative, and judicial branches of government, as well as in the management of public and private enterprises. I chose to focus on this instead of just tracking national parliaments because it offers a more comprehensive and representative view of women's leadership across the board.

Methods

The methodology for this project involved selecting a relevant indicator from the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) database and processing it into a visual format. I chose to focus on SDG Target 5.5, which aims to ensure women's full and effective participation and equal opportunities for leadership. To map this, I selected indicator 5.5.2 - the proportion of women in managerial positions. To build the map, I downloaded the official dataset from the UN Database and cleaned it. I then used QGIS to import the data as vector layers and edited their properties. Most of the time, I spent working with the attribute table properties to link statistical data to the map. For the visual design, I created a color palette that alludes to femininity (especially in Western context) but still remains neutral by using the color pink. I made sure it ensures accessibility for people with visual impairments, including colorblindness. I also used a specific bold border style to explicitly highlight countries where women occupy 50% or more of managerial roles.

Results & Findings

The final choropleth map successfully displays the global distribution of women in senior and middle management roles for the year 2023. By mapping the data on a country-wide scale, distinct regional trends and data hotspots are clearly visible. The map shows that while many nations still have low to moderate percentages of women in leadership, several countries stand out as significant hotspots where women hold the majority of managerial positions. While these national averages effectively highlight countries making progress towards fulfilling target 5.5, they also show many data gaps, with many countries not having data on this indicator, making it not fully representative and comparable Furthermore, because the data is presented at a macro level, it likely masks internal regional disproportions variations between urban and rural areas, and does not show underlying sector correlations, like whether a high percentage is driven by a systemic push or a heavy economic reliance on the healthcare and service industries and fields traditionally occupied by women.
